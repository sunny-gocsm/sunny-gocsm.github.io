What we're building

Two surfaces, one engine

Everything operates on the Account (one HL sub-account) and a shared criteria/matching engine that answers “who matches right now / how many / who's about to.” Two surfaces sit on top.

🔔 Attention action layer

“What needs me right now?” A live queue of sub-accounts needing a play + two hero numbers (N need attention, $X MRR at risk), plus a “Step in” list for accounts a play ran on but didn't fix.

📘 Playbooks systems layer

A growing library (~57 today, growing toward hundreds) of pre-built automations: browse → set up (3 steps) → run on the right accounts. Each play = a HighLevel workflow + an audience. This is the core product.

Epics E3–E6 · page /playbooks · embed /embed/playbooks
The trial wedge — sell the system, not the insight. GoCSM converts a trial by getting the owner to turn on automations (playbooks) during the trial — activating the system is what sells, not surfacing insights. So day one introduces no new concepts: it surfaces only HL-native problems the owner already knows (failed payments, no-login, …) plus the two actions; the coined Health layer stays gated as later depth/monetization. (§1, §4.4)
The product stance: GoCSM diagnoses; HighLevel executes. There are exactly two outward actions — Trigger Workflow Request Feedback — both run as HighLevel workflows. One playbook maps 1:1 to one HL workflow; GoCSM owns the audience + lifecycle and records what was tried per account (the closed-loop “tried but didn't fix it” signal that feeds Step-in), while HighLevel owns sending. (§4.7, Epic E7)

The most important cross-cutting rule

Phase 1 vs Phase 2 — the Health gate ★

Health is a gated, coined scoring system. Every surface must work in two phases, and the gate is enforced in the product's data layer (a trust rule, not cosmetic — not merely hidden in the UI; NFR-4).

Phase 1 — Health NOT configured default / trial · the majority state

Fully functional on its own, using only HL-native data.

  • Available: logins, payment/billing status, workflow usage, plan, renewal date, feature adoption, raw NPS, MRR/spend, account.priority
  • Hidden: Health score, Health bands (Thriving/Healthy/Watch/At-Risk), lifecycle stages
  • Hidden: Health-derived queue signals, the rich match preview (band/breadth/forecast)

Phase 2 — Health configured additive, never a prerequisite

Unlocks additively — nothing that worked in Phase 1 changes or breaks.

  • Adds: Health score & bands, lifecycle stages, the “Health & Risk” filter group
  • Adds: Health-derived AttentionSignals, the 7-day forecast, the audience makeup, band-aware previews
  • Still never exposed (either phase): PAS & raw pillar scores — never filterable, internal only
⚠️ The rule: a single per-agency Health-config flag drives every branch. In Phase 1 the product strips all health.* fields, bands, lifecycle, pillars from every response — catalog, accounts, queue, previews, restatement, copy — in the data layer, not merely in the UI. The marketplace “Situation” rating uses plain words that deliberately avoid the Health-band terms for exactly this reason.
Concepts & objects (§5 — canonical product model)

Domain model at a glance

All entities are agency-scoped (multi-tenant; account ids unique within an agency). “Live account” = enabled & not churned.

Account core
11 sub-objects: identity, ownership, status, lifecycle*, pipeline, health*, login, adoption, revenue, feedback, onboarding. (* = gated)
Signal
Event log. A critical setup actively being lost (a sticky reversal within 30 days) → the “save” plays.
Playbook growing library
A concrete automation for one specific problem: title, problem, does, pre-written actions, category, effort, Situation rating, a default trigger, + a ~1-min video & an editable HL workflow.
Recipe ×6
A pre-seeded criteria set + linked play; seeds the builder's empty state.
CriteriaSet
The audience query: a flat list of conditions (field · plain-word operator · value), optionally with one level of any-of/all-of grouping.
AttentionSignal
A named (query + play + priority) that becomes a queue row. Tier: native or health.
Attempt
A play ran but didn't fix — powers the “Step in” list. Status flat/worse/unconfirmed · confidence high/low.
Autopilot + OverseeMode
Per-agency deployment: off → on → paused → archived, plus governance auto / ease / review.
WorkflowDraft
Autosaved setup-in-progress, keyed by recipe (step, criteria, “workflow ready”, saved-at).
Criteria catalog (§5.2): 30 fields · 8 groups · ~7 “common”. Operators are plain words, never symbols (e.g. is more than, in the next, is any of). An unknown field id ⇒ the condition is simply ignored (drop, never error). The full field & operator universe — and each play's default Simple-view filters — lives in playbooks-catalog.json.
Epic E6 · §5.7

Playbook lifecycle (state machine)

Per-agency, idempotent transitions. No hard deletes of a play that ever ran — soft archive only; restore always lands in paused (never directly live).

off — enable(oversee) → on ⇄ pause / resume ⇄ paused — archive → archived — restore → paused

Each transition has an exact confirmation toast (e.g. “Playbook paused”, “Archived — history kept”, “Restored — back in Paused”). Turning a play fully off exists but is not offered for plays with run history. Each deployment is listed by state to drive the “Your playbooks” management view.

Epic E4 · the heart of activation

The unified 3-step setup & activation flow

One reusable flow opened from the marketplace, the Attention queue, or the Accounts table. Persistent header + stepper + footer. Autosaves a draft on every change (resume: “Picked up where you left off”).

1
What it does · Action
A ~1-min video + the play's plain actions + the editable HL workflow to open, modify & brand (logo, colors, custom variables). The owner acknowledges completion — “I've completed this” — which is verified published before Continue unlocks.
2
When & who it runs on · Trigger
The trigger builder (E5), seeded from the play's default criteria (Phase-1 strips Health). Live match count; Continue locked at 0 matches.
3
Review & publish
Two plain-English summaries + account preview (P2 rows / P1 count) + Autopilot toggle (default ON) → Publish.
Ordered & gated — nothing fires until Review. The sequence is Action (set up & publish the workflow) → Trigger (who it runs on) → Review → only then does it fire. Each step's gate must pass before the next; the owner himself acknowledges the workflow is set up (rather than the product polling HighLevel), and publish is the single moment of arming.
Publish semantics: ongoing + autopilot ON ⇒ runs continuously on new matches; autopilot OFF ⇒ a one-time run over current matches; the fixed-selection variant (opened with pre-picked accounts) runs ONCE on exactly those, no autopilot toggle. Publishing twice is a no-op (never double-deploys). Draft cleared only on success.
Epic E5 · the engine + the UX

The trigger / audience builder — two modes

Deliberately simple for activation. A live plain-English restatement sits at the top at all times (“Runs on accounts where…”), and a live count + $ at risk (“$8,400 at risk · 5 accounts”) gives instant social proof. The mode toggle is non-destructive. Every play ships default criteria, shown in plain English, so the owner can publish without building anything.

Simple default · no AI

  • A tiny, relevant quick-add: plan + priority as the universal base, plus the 1–2 filters most relevant to that play (playbook-aware). Tap to add a ready-made, editable condition.
  • An already-used field is hidden; “Browse all fields” opens the full categorized + searchable picker.
  • Flat conditions + one Match all | any switch.

Advanced describe-in-words + rule builder

  • Describe your audience in plain words → editable rules. Production uses real AI (a one-time cost per activation); the prototype is deterministic, behind the same contract. It never auto-commits — “always check them” honesty.
  • A nested boolean builder: Match all/any · Add condition · Add group (one level only).
  • Switching back to Simple is disabled when genuine nesting exists — never silently flatten/reset.
💡 The live count is a disclosure: “N of your accounts match right now · See who” → Phase 1 shows a plain name + $/mo list (no Health); Phase 2 shows a richer preview (bands, a breadth meter, a 7-day forecast).

§8 · what the product must be able to do

Capabilities the product must expose

Everything is agency-scoped and phase-filtered before it reaches the owner — no Health value ever surfaces in Phase 1. This is what the product can do, not how it's wired.

Accounts & audiences

  • List the agency's accounts, show any one account's full detail (incl. a churned account), and an account's signal history (newest first).
  • Answer the standing selectors: who has a failed payment, whose onboarding is stalled, who is losing a critical setup, who renews within a window.
  • Give the top-line rollup: total accounts, live accounts, total MRR, MRR at risk.
  • For any audience: answer “who matches now / how many”, restate it in plain English, and turn a plain-words description into editable rules (real AI in production).
  • (Phase 2) Answer “who's likely to match within ~7 days” (with a confidence cue) + the audience makeup by band and plan.

Library, setup & lifecycle

  • Browse the play library with faceted filtering, search, and sorting, with live facet counts.
  • Show one play's detail and its own impact for the agency; surface a single recommended “start here” play.
  • Save and resume an in-progress setup, and discard it.
  • Publish a play ongoing (handles new matches over time) or run-once (a fixed set) — idempotently (publishing twice never double-deploys).
  • Pause / resume / archive / restore a deployment (soft archive, no hard delete); record + read the escalation preference.

Non-functional must-haves (§7)

match count feels instant near-real-time account freshness phase gate in the data layer strict agency isolation idempotent publish & lifecycle no hard deletes describe→rules never auto-commits one bad condition ⇒ no-match, not failure auditable transitions accessible & localizable
§9.A — why this wins (unoccupied white space)

The differentiators to protect in the build

1 · Live plain-English restatement. A generated sentence of the user's own rule — no competitor, not even HighLevel, shows this. Don't reduce it to a query summary.
2 · Live “who matches now” count + $ at risk + glanceable preview, updating on every edit, on a non-technical surface.
3 · Describe-in-words → editable rules + live count. The AI drafts; the owner lands in an editable builder (never a black box) with the count as ground truth.
4 · Point-of-use AI honesty (“always check them”), where competitors bury the disclaimer.
